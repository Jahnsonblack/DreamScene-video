Supplementary Results: DreamScene vs Baselines

This page shows the qualitative comparison of DreamScene against four baselines: Text2Room, Text2NeRF, ProlificDreamer and Set-the-Scene. For each prompt, we show RGB videos followed by the corresponding depth videos, generated using the official code provided by each method. We adopt a unified camera trajectory for all methods to enable fair and meaningful comparison. Specifically, we conduct testing on the same scenes used during training, but replace the original training camera poses with a continuous trajectory that simulates natural human exploration behavior: the camera starts from the scene center, first moves along straight paths in multiple directions across the scene, and then performs a circular motion around the center, with the radius of the circular path set to two-thirds of the scene diameter. As shown in the videos, inpainting-based methods (e.g., Text2Room and Text2NeRF) resemble stitched panoramas rather than consistent 3D representaions, lacking geometric consistency and often collapsing under novel camera views. ProlificDreamer is designed for object-centric generation and struggles with scene-wide 3D consistency, leading to noticeably degraded rendering quality in complex environments. Set-the-Scene improves structural consistency but remains constrained by the low visual fidelity inherent to SDS-based generation. Crucially, none of these methods support fully automated scene generation or flexible object-level editing. In contrast, DreamScene unifies automated layout planning, robust 3D structure, high visual quality, and controllable scene editing into a scalable and practical pipeline.
